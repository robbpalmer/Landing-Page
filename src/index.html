About Me

Hello and thank you for taking a look.

My name is Rob Palmer and I'm a budding developer currently based in Portland, OR.

I spent my career in the financial sales world, but it's time for a transition. I've gotten the opportunity to learn about the investment universe while developing my communication skills.

I found a good deal of success here, which you may see in my resume. It was a fulfilling career, but the ladder was relatively short, and sales can only be so intellectually stimulating. I knew I wanted more out of my career. After many conversations with those closest to me, I decided to move on. I rented out my home, sold 90% of my belongings and went traveling. I backpacked through Central America, joined a band on a small island in Panama, taught English in Prague, along with many other adventures (I'd be happy to chat about!).

Along this journey, I discovered the options available to learn how to become a developer.

Some close friends of mine are developers, who of course tout the benefits of working in this world, encouraged me to give it a shot. They would say things like "You'd be really good at this," or "You've got the brain for it." I began my journey with a program called "Learn JavaScript the Hard Way" taught by Zed A. Shaw.

I quickly saw the appeal and found lots of enjoyment in the problem solving process. I joined a coursera course where I recieved a certificate in full-stack development, went through the freecodecamp.org classes on front-end development, and most recently have been utilizing Treehouse as a resource for learning and practicing.

I've realized (and am excited by) the fact that there is always more to learn . I'm motivated by accomplishment and progress. I'm thrilled with the discovery that this is something I enjoy, and my current goal is to get my foot in the door with a company who can find good use for a motivated, disciplined, life-long-learner with a passion for improvement and success .

Current Project

Tutorials, tutorials, tutorials...

I realized after a short while that the best way for me to challenge myself was to make something on my own. One of my favorite browser extensions is Momentum, a customized homepage. It's a weather app, clock, personal greeting, quote generator, background image generator, and a todo-list all in one, so I figured recreating this on my own with my newfound knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and React would allow me to learn quickly, along with showcase several things at once. It's my fake version of Momentum.

I'm calling it Fakementum.
